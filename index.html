The AEIF Data Collection: A Dataset for Infrastructure-Supported Perception Research with Focus on Public Transportation

Marcel Vosshans1,2, Alexander Baumann1, Matthias Drueppel3, Omar Ait-Aider2, Ralf Woerner1, Youcef Mezouar2, Thao Dang1 Markus Enzweiler1
1University of Esslingen 2Clermont Auvergne INP / CNRS 3Cooperative State University Stuttgart
Paper arXiv Code  Google Colab

Abstract

We present our vision and ongoing work for a novel dataset designed to advance research into the interoperability of intelligent vehicles and infrastructure, specifically aimed at enhancing cooperative perception and interaction in the realm of public transportation.

Unlike conventional datasets centered on ego-vehicle data, this approach encompasses both a stationary sensor tower and a moving vehicle, each equipped with cameras, LiDARs, and GNSS, while the vehicle additionally includes an inertial navigation system. Our setup features comprehensive calibration and time synchronization, ensuring seamless and accurate sensor data fusion crucial for studying complex, dynamic scenes. Emphasizing public transportation, the dataset targets to include scenes like bus station maneuvers and driving on dedicated bus lanes, reflecting the specifics of small public buses. We introduce the open-source .4mse file format for the new dataset, accompanied by a research kit. This kit provides tools such as ego-motion compensation or LiDAR-to-camera projection enabling advanced research on intelligent vehicle-infrastructure integration. Our approach does not include annotations; however, we plan to implement automatically generated labels sourced from state-of-the-art public repositories.

Setup

Ego-vehicle (sensor bus):

A stereo camera and a top LiDAR are centrally mounted, with the stereo camera located inside the bus, behind the windshield. The top LiDAR has a 45° horizontal FoV, designed to cover the camera's entire view, including above the horizon. Two additional LiDARs, mounted at the front corners, are angled to cover blind spots. An INS, combining GNSS and IMU data, supports navigation, with RTK correction from the sensor tower for precise positioning.

Sensor tower:

The static sensor tower supports interoperability studies with a simple, modular design, featuring a 3-meter pole stabilized by weights. It is powered by batteries and a solar panel and equipped with LTE/5G and a V2X roadside unit for communication. A 360° LiDAR and GNSS antennas are mounted at the top, while two adjustable camera-LiDAR units are positioned higher up, providing flexible sensing for each scene.

3D-RGB Pointclouds

We offer 3D RGB point clouds generated by integrating data from LiDAR and cameras, providing detailed spatial and color information for enhanced scene understanding.

Infrastructure Camera-Lidar

A key component of the dataset is the viewpoint from the observing infrastructure, providing a comprehensive perspective on the environment.

Stereo Data

We provide a high-quality stereo camera system designed for accurate depth perception and 3D imaging, ideal for advanced computer vision applications.
